Since 1912

A century and more in the service of God and country.

What began as a small parochial school under Spanish Jesuits has grown, across more than a hundred years, into a university educating Zamboanga's grade schoolers, high schoolers, and undergraduates alike.

1912

Escuela Catolica

Spanish Jesuits open Escuela Catolica near the Immaculate Conception Church — the site now commemorated by Zamboanga's Sunken Garden. Fr. Miguel Saderra Mata helps subsidize the young school, whose first classes are held in a wooden building that doubled as the garage of the Mindanao Transit, near the old Plaza de Don Juan de Salcedo — known today as Plaza Pershing.

1928

The High School Opens

Ateneo High School is established, offering primary and secondary education for boys. A school band forms soon after, playing at graduation ceremonies — the earliest root of today's AdZU Concert Band.

1932

First Graduates

The city government formally recognizes the high school, and its first class crosses the stage the same year, with Roseller T. Lim as valedictorian — the start of a lineage of alumni now generations deep.

1952

College Is Born

The school expands into tertiary education, opening its doors to collegiate formation for the young men and women of Western Mindanao.

2001

University Status

Nearly a century after its founding, the Ateneo de Zamboanga is formally recognized as a university — now home to schools of law, medicine, nursing, and graduate studies.

Today

Five Divisions, One Mission

AdZU forms students across grade school, junior and senior high school, college, and graduate and professional programs — still under the patronage of Our Lady of the Immaculate Conception and St. Ignatius of Loyola, and still true to the motto it was founded on.

Read the Crest

The seal, explained.

Every line of the AdZU seal carries meaning — inherited from the family of St. Ignatius of Loyola, and adapted to the shores of Western Mindanao. Tap each mark to hear what it stands for.

01 — The Jesuit Monogram

The letters IHS are the first three letters of the name of Jesus in Greek — Iota, Eta, Sigma. Within it rest the cross and nails, recalling Christ's Passion.

02 — The Glorious Sunburst

Rays of blue and white emanate from the Holy Name, signifying grace and gifts extended to all who pass through the Ateneo's doors.

03 — The Coat of Arms

Red diagonal stripes on a field of gold, drawn from the Oñaz family — the maternal line of St. Ignatius of Loyola himself.

04 — The Cross and the Vinta

The cross marks the school's Christian character; the vinta sail beside it stands for the diverse cultures of Western Mindanao it was built to serve.

05 — The Ribbons

The crown ribbon carries the motto, "Pro Deo et Patria." The base ribbon carries the school's name — Ateneo de Zamboanga.

Three Sites, Zamboanga City

Rooted on both sides of Zamboanga City.

From the main gates on La Purisima Street to the grade-school grounds of Tumaga — here's where every division of AdZU calls home.

Main Campus

Salvador Campus

The heart of AdZU — home to Senior High School, College, and the Schools of Law, Medicine, Nursing, and Graduate Studies. Spanish colonial-inspired buildings sit alongside modern halls, shaded walkways, and open courtyards, just steps from where Escuela Catolica first began.
